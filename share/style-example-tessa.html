Backstory

Tessa grew up in a traveling caravan where she learned to charm coin from pockets and applause from crowds in equal measure. After a brush with a hag who tried to barter for her voice, she swore off bargains with anything that doesn't first take a deep breath. Tessa grew up in a traveling caravan where she learned to charm coin from pockets and applause from crowds in equal measure. After a brush with a hag who tried to barter for her voice, she swore off bargains with anything that doesn't first take a deep breath. The caravan was her family in everything but blood — her actual parents had given her up to the troupe at five, and she remembers them only as silhouettes against firelight. Her foster mother, Old Wren, taught her the lute; her foster father, Bram, taught her how to read a room. By the time she was sixteen she was the troupe's headline act. By eighteen she'd been charged with theft in two duchies and acquitted in both — the second time by performing for the judge. The hag was the turning point. She had been camped alone outside a village called Hollowmere when an old woman approached her fire and offered three songs in exchange for a single one of Tessa's. Tessa, half-asleep, almost said yes. Something — luck, instinct, the tug of her grandmother's old halfling charm against her chest — made her hesitate. She declined, fled, and woke the next morning to find every bird in a half-mile radius dead in the grass around her bedroll. She has not slept easy since.

Personality

Quick-witted, performatively flippant, deeply guarded. Tessa flirts to deflect, jokes to disarm, and only opens up to people who have proven they aren't keeping score.

Bonds, Ideals, Flaws

  • Bond: Old Wren raised her. If Wren ever needs her, no contract or obligation comes first.
  • Ideal: Honesty is overrated. Performance is honest enough.
  • Flaw: When cornered, she lies even to allies — a reflex from a decade of running.

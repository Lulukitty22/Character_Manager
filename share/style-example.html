When material focus or elemental sources are unavailable, spells can burn Spell Memories instead. Roll the relevant memory-cost formula (1d4 × 1d8 − CHA) and subtract from this pool. CHA is currently +0; provisional ruling may treat it as 2.

Personality

Hostile, extremely guarded, and nearly mute unless forced to speak. Elfnein assumes others want to hurt her, exploit her, or steal from her. She is an outcast whose sanity and memory have eroded over centuries.

Backstory

Elfnein was born in Arnica more than 600 years ago. Her father, an alchemist and doctor, cured a plague through science and alchemy, but the state branded him a witch and burned him alive in front of her. The trauma broke her. Elfnein was born in Arnica more than 600 years ago. Her father, an alchemist and doctor, cured a plague through science and alchemy, but the state branded him a witch because he lacked status and was seen practicing religious or arcane arts. He was burned alive in front of her. The trauma broke her. She interpreted her father's lesson to dissect the world literally, pursuing alchemy and arcane transmutation down to the structure of matter in a desperate attempt to bring him back. She slaughtered Arnica in rage, tried to equivalent-exchange souls for a soul, and spent centuries surviving through forbidden rebirth and memory-burning alchemy. She has no idea that magic is no longer forbidden.

DM / Table Notes

Provisional sheet. Elfnein is less mechanically settled than Capella or Seraphine, so uncertain features are marked with Provisional, Future, or DM Fiat chips. Current assumed play state is Wizard 9 / Sorcerer 1, total level 10. Future Wizard/Sorcerer progression listed as available planning rather than current access.

Her magic is flavoured as alchemy. When material focus or elemental sources are unavailable, spells can burn Spell Memories. The Memory Burn formula (1d4 × 1d8 − CHA) treats CHA as +0 by RAW, but the source notes reference CHA(2). Use whichever the table prefers; flag the inconsistency at session start.

Open Questions

  • Bladesong AC: uses INT instead of CHA per Bladesinger variant. Confirm with DM each session.
  • Shield + Bladesong: table ruling 25, raw math implies 27. Currently kept at 25.
  • Faust Robe: not currently accessible. Transformation rolls exist (1d20+3, 1d12+2 if interrupted) but are reserved.
  • Sanity: centuries of memory-burning have eroded continuity. Long-term roleplay implication, not a mechanical penalty (yet).
